Unagi Chazuke

Description

A light and tasty main dish. The tea and soup weave an elegant, fragrant melody punctuated by the pure white grains, while the unagi, baptized in coal-fire, provides tender, gentle decoration to the mix. The various aromas of eel and tea and the light sweetness of rice mix well on your tongue into something truly sublime. A triple threat not to be trifled with, indeed.

Ingredients

  • Eel Meat
  • Rice
  • Seagrass
  • Salt

Steps

  1. If eel is unseasoned, marinate the eel with the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar for at least one hour
  2. Portion the eel into 4-5 inch sections
  3. Line a baking tray with aluminum foil and place the eel onto the tray
  4. Cover with aluminum foil
  5. Bake at 375 degrees F or 190 degrees C for 15 minutes
  6. Remove from the oven and take off the top layer of aluminum foil
  7. Set oven to broil and broil the eel, uncovered, for 2-3 minutes
  8. For the nori, fold the sheet and cut the nori into small strips
  9. For the green tea, brew the tea according to the package instructions with hot water
